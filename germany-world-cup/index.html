Germany's Run to the World Cup Victory

by Aja Ellison

The German men's national soccer team did not lose a single game on the road to winning the 2014 World Cup, their fourth title. This story -- told through graphics -- documents their path to victory.

Germany Got Better as Tournaent Progressed

Germany tied only one game en route to winning the World Cup -- a surprise result against Ghana -- but they did face several scares. They required extra-time to beat Algeria in the quarter-finals and took 120 minutes to defeat Argentina in the final.

Stadium Hosting Wins

Germany played seven games in six different stadiums en route to winning their fourth World Cup in 2014, traveling hundreds of miles across Brazil. They won six games and drew one, 2-2 against Ghana on June 21.

How Germany Won

Germany won the World Cup by controlling the ball with more crisp passes than any other team in the tournament, taking more high percentage shots and scoring buckets of goals while keeping their opponents from finding the back of the net.

Germany demonstrated outstanding ball movement, which led them to taking great shots. Germany averaged 726.29 passes per game, almost doubling the amount of passes averaged by the other components.

Germany's was very efficient with there passing completion. Germany led the passing percentage with an impressive passing completion percentage of 82%.

The team dominated as they have given themselves both abundant and efficent scoring opportunities. Germany averaged nearly 16 shots per game in which 10 of those shots were on target.

The difference in goals for and goals aganst had a major impact on Germany's World Cup victory. Theier difference in goals for and goals against was 14.

Germany dominated in the tournament defensively as well. The men protected thier goal with an average of 13 clearances per game.

Germany's deffense made it nearly impossible for teams to make plays across court. The team won an average of 13 tackles per game.

A Passing Machine

Germany moved the ball around the field at a rapid clip throughout the tournament, passing the ball on average of 726 times per game--well above the median of 513 passes for all 32 teams.
